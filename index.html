The Brief

Investors fund the vision. Nobody installs the engine.

The gap between a funded founder and a functioning company is almost never about the idea. It is almost never about the product or the market. It is about the fact that nobody taught them how to run the thing — and everyone around them is too invested in the story to say so.

OpInt goes in when that gap becomes a crisis. We read the operating environment, diagnose what is actually broken, and build the infrastructure that turns a funded idea into a company that can survive contact with reality.

Not as consultants. Not as coaches. As operators who have spent 17 years inside the engine rooms of complex institutions across East and Central Africa — and know exactly what breaks, and when.

Known Failure Patterns
What We See
01
The Founder Is The System

Every decision routes through one person. Every process lives in their head. They are the bottleneck wearing the CEO badge — and the company cannot move faster than they can think.

02
Motion Mistaken For Movement

The calendar is full. The team is busy. The investor updates look good. But nothing critical is actually advancing. Velocity is a feeling. Progress is a number. Most founders cannot tell the difference.

03
The Gap After The Cheque

The round closes and the chaos doubles. New board, new obligations, new stakeholders — and zero operating infrastructure to manage any of it. This is when companies die quietly.

I have spent 17 years being the person who sees everything, fixes everything, and hands the credit back to the principal. I am structurally incapable of needing the spotlight. That is, counterintuitively, the single biggest advantage I can offer a founder whose biggest fear is being overshadowed by the people they hire.

Engagement Architecture
The Method
Phase 01
The Diagnostic

Five to seven days embedded. No presentations. No workshops. We read the operating environment — the decisions, the dynamics, the dysfunction — and produce a clear picture of what is actually broken and why.

5 — 7 Days
Phase 02
The Intervention

Thirty to ninety days building the infrastructure that should have been there from day one. Decision frameworks. Delegation architecture. Operating rhythms. The systems that let a founder work on the company instead of inside it.

30 — 90 Days
Phase 03
The Hold

Optional. Three months post-intervention to ensure the new architecture holds, the team has absorbed it, and the founder is no longer the single point of failure. Then we leave.
