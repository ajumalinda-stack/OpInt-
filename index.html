What Breaks At Series A
Known Failure Patterns
01
The Founder Is The System

Every decision routes through one person. Every process lives in their head. At Series A this was charming. At Series B it is a red flag. Investors do not back single points of failure.

02
The Blind Pilot Problem

The founder cannot tell you their exact burn multiple or cost to acquire a customer without opening five different apps. The data is scattered, the reporting is manual, and they are flying through thick fog.

03
The Silo Stagnation

Sales promises what product has not built. Support does not know what sales told the customer. Departments do not talk and the customer bears the cost. The leaky bucket stays leaky because no one owns the pipe.

04
The Gap Before The Round

Six months before Series B and the operations are held together by duct tape. Investors will look under the hood. Banks will audit. Acquirers will send consultants. What they find will determine your valuation.
